COVID-19 Flight Data Research

This was a two-semester project with the Purdue Datamine and Sandia National Laboratories to investigate flight data before and during the COVID-19 pandemic.

I was on a team of 3 within the sandia team that wanted to investigate whether the pandemic resulted in any changes to the flight trajectories and whether the lengths of flights in general, in terms of distance and time, had greatly changed.

During the first semester, we spent most of our time researching and cleaning the large OpenSky data set that we were going to use for our analysis. The data was cleaned using R and we began to prepare the visualizations in Python.

In the second semester, we worked to finalize our renderings and report our conclusions at the symposium held in the spring. The cleaned data was rendered in Python using Pandas and scripts for calculations were made using NumPy.

All of the publicly-available findings from this project can be viewed here from the e-symposium.
